---
title: PREPIC2 — IVC filters added to anticoagulation (JAMA 2015)
url: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=PREPIC2+vena+cava+filter
type: study
---
PREPIC2 was a randomized trial testing whether adding a retrievable inferior vena cava (IVC) filter to standard anticoagulation reduced recurrent pulmonary embolism in patients with acute symptomatic PE who also had additional risk factors and who could be anticoagulated. Patients were randomized to anticoagulation plus a retrievable filter versus anticoagulation alone, with the filter intended for retrieval at three months.

The trial found no significant reduction in recurrent PE at three months and no mortality benefit from adding the filter on top of anticoagulation. This is the key teaching point: in a patient who can be anticoagulated, a filter does not add meaningful protection and should not be placed routinely.

The earlier PREPIC trial had shown that permanent filters reduced early PE but increased deep vein thrombosis over long-term follow-up, with no mortality benefit. Together the PREPIC studies anchor the principle that IVC filters substitute for anticoagulation when it is contraindicated; they are not an add-on for extra protection, and an unretrieved filter is a source of long-term harm.
